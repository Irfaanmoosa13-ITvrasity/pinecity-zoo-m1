GEMSBOK

Gemsbok are herbivores. They consume grass, thorny shrubs, roots, tubers and supplement their water intake by eating wild tsama melons and cucumbers. Gemsboks prefer rocky plains but can survive in rocky hills and in areas as dry as the Kalahari Desert. Gemsboks live in herds of 10 to 40 animals and can live up to 20 years. a female Gemsbok can give birth to a single clf at about 2 and a half years. A gemsbok weights on average about 204 kg and stands about 1.2 meters tall.
